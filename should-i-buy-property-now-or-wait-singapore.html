Buy Property Now vs Wait (Singapore, 2026)

Updated: February 2026

Timing is not about predicting the next 12 months. It’s: are you buying a stable home plan or a fragile bet that collapses under shocks?

Stress test affordability All comparisons

Decision checklist

  • Horizon: Will you hold for 5–10 years (or might you sell soon)?
  • Buffers: Do you have 6–12 months runway after downpayment?
  • Rate shock: Can you survive +1–2% rates and higher costs together?
  • Life plan: Is your job/school/family plan stable enough to commit?
  • Alternatives: Is renting acceptable while you build buffers and clarity?

Buy now vs wait: what changes the answer

  • Your horizon: if you might sell in <5 years, timing mistakes are amplified by transaction costs.
  • Cashflow fragility: if a rate shock breaks you, “buy now” is not brave — it’s brittle.
  • Price vs rate trade: a “cheaper price” can be offset by a higher rate (and vice versa). You need an exposure lens.
  • Life optionality: if your job/location/family plan is uncertain, waiting can be rational even if the math says “buy”.

Decision rules (Singapore reality)

  • Buy now if you have buffer, long horizon, and the purchase improves your life even under conservative assumptions.
  • Wait if you are stretching affordability, you lack buffer, or your life plan is unstable (moving schools/jobs, family timing).
  • If you’re “waiting for the perfect bottom”, you’re usually just trading one risk (price) for another (rate/opportunity cost).

FAQ

Is it better to buy property now or wait in 2026?

There isn’t a universal answer. The right choice depends on your horizon, buffers, and whether you remain safe under rate/expense shocks. If you pass a conservative stress test, buying now can be reasonable.

Should I wait for interest rates to drop?

Rates may drop or stay higher for longer. If you can only buy when rates drop, you’re already too stretched. Use a stress test and buffer rule instead of forecasting.

Should I wait for prices to fall?

Prices can fall, but transaction costs and opportunity cost matter. If you need a home and plan to hold long-term, the bigger risk is buying a fragile budget.

What’s the simplest rule?

If you have buffer, stable plan, and you can hold 5–10 years: buy when you find the right unit at a sane price. If you’re stretched or uncertain: wait and build buffers.
