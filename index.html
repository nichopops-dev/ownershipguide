What Ownership Guide does

Ownership Guide is a Singapore “decision engine” for expensive, long‑term choices: cars, homes, and financing. We translate confusing trade‑offs into one comparable unit (monthly cost, total interest, cash vs CPF, break‑even years), so you can decide with numbers instead of vibes.

Start with one of these high‑intent paths

  • Buying a home: Upfront cash + CPF → Max home loan (TDSR/MSR) → Stamp duty (BSD/ABSD)
  • Selling and upgrading: Sell → Buy Next pipeline (prefilled tools) → Upgrade / downgrade bridge
  • Car decision: Affordability → True monthly cost → Lease vs buy

How to interpret calculators

Most tools here are planning models. They make assumptions explicit so you can pressure‑test scenarios quickly. For final numbers, confirm with your bank/lawyer/agent and official sources.

Why the site looks the way it does

We intentionally separate decision guides (the reasoning) from calculators (the numbers). A good decision is usually stable across small changes in assumptions. If a decision flips with minor tweaks, it means you should gather better inputs (valuation, rent, fees) before acting.

References

  • Stamp duties: IRAS (BSD/ABSD)
  • CPF refund mechanics: CPF Board (accrued interest)
  • Borrowing limits: MAS rules / bank credit assessment (TDSR/MSR)

FAQ

Is this financial advice?

No. These are simplified decision models for planning and comparison. Use them to ask better questions and verify with professionals.

Why do some pages have both a guide and a calculator?

The guide explains the reasoning and the calculator gives the numbers. Using both reduces mistakes.

How often is the site updated?

We update pages when policies/rates change and when readers report issues. Key pages show a “last updated” note.
