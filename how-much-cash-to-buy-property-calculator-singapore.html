Common mistakes

  • Mixing up cash vs CPF (they behave differently in approvals and refunds).
  • Using optimistic assumptions (resale value, appreciation, investment return) without testing a conservative scenario.
  • Forgetting one-off costs (fees, penalties, duties) that dominate short horizons.

FAQ

Why does the cash number jump so much when ABSD is included?

ABSD is a large one-off tax, often cash-heavy depending on CPF usage and timing.

Does option fee always have to be cash?

Typically yes. Treat it as cash-first planning unless you have a confirmed structure otherwise.

Is this ‘minimum cash’ guaranteed?

No. Your bank’s valuation and approval can change LTV; plan a buffer.

How to use the result wisely

Because the mortgage dominates attention. People focus on loan size and monthly instalment, but transactions often become stressful because the upfront cash stack was undercounted. This page is designed to make those costs more visible early.

Why buyers underestimate cash so often

Buying property in Singapore involves more than downpayment alone. Cash needed can include a cash portion of the downpayment, stamp duties, legal fees, valuation/admin fees, option-related payments, and a practical buffer for moving or renovation. This calculator helps you estimate how much cash you may need so you do not confuse “loan eligibility” with “cash readiness”.

What this calculator estimates

The aim is not perfection. It is to avoid being surprised by costs you could have anticipated with a slightly better planning process.

  • Use a conservative scenario, not only a comfortable scenario.
  • Test whether the decision still works if one major assumption worsens.
  • Write down what the calculator excludes so you do not treat the result as complete.
  • Prefer a slightly pessimistic planning number over an optimistic one.

How to improve the quality of your estimate

If the cash number leaves you almost empty after completion, the purchase may technically work but still be too aggressive. Good property decisions usually leave room for repairs, moving friction, and a normal financial life after the purchase.

How to use this result with discipline

A buyer can be loan-eligible and still be cash-unready. That mismatch creates stress late in the transaction. This calculator is useful because it closes that blind spot and forces you to think about liquidity, not just debt capacity.

Cash readiness vs loan readiness

Cash readiness makes the transaction smoother and gives you more negotiating power. Buyers who are technically able to buy but cash-stretched often end up making worse trade-offs later in the process.
