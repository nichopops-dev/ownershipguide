How to use this

This page is a planning model. Use it to get a directionally correct answer fast, then confirm final numbers with your bank/lawyer/agent and official calculators where relevant.

Key assumptions (what this model does and doesn’t do)

  • This estimates minimum upfront outlay using your LTV and min cash downpayment assumptions.
  • BSD/ABSD are major drivers; confirm with IRAS and your conveyancing lawyer if your profile is non-standard.
  • CPF payment ability depends on CPF balances and CPF usage rules; this model treats CPF as available if you choose so.

Worked example

Example: S$1.2M purchase with 75% LTV and 5% min cash downpayment

A common planning view: cash is needed for (1) minimum cash downpayment, (2) any cash-only fees, and (3) stamp duties if not CPF-paid. Use this to avoid “I can afford the loan but not the completion cash” surprises.

Common mistakes

  • Mixing up cash vs CPF (they behave differently in approvals and refunds).
  • Using optimistic assumptions (resale value, appreciation, investment return) without testing a conservative scenario.
  • Forgetting one-off costs (fees, penalties, duties) that dominate short horizons.

FAQ

Why does the cash number jump so much when ABSD is included?

ABSD is a large one-off tax, often cash-heavy depending on CPF usage and timing.

Does option fee always have to be cash?

Typically yes. Treat it as cash-first planning unless you have a confirmed structure otherwise.

Is this ‘minimum cash’ guaranteed?

No. Your bank’s valuation and approval can change LTV; plan a buffer.
