Methodology & assumptions

Inputs explained

  • Loan amount: the principal you borrow from the bank (exclude downpayment and fees).
  • Interest rate (% p.a.): the annual rate. For floating loans, this changes over time — use a conservative stress‑test rate for planning.
  • Loan tenure (years): how long you take to repay. Longer tenure lowers monthly payment but increases total interest.
  • Extra payments (if any): optional lumpsum or monthly top‑ups that reduce principal faster (check your loan terms for penalties).

How to interpret the results

  • Monthly payment: what you pay each month under the current assumptions.
  • Total interest: total interest paid over the full tenure (this is what tenure and rate mainly drive).
  • Amortization schedule: early years are interest‑heavy; later years shift toward principal repayment.
  • Break‑even comparisons: use this schedule alongside refinance/reprice tools to see if fees are worth it.

Common mistakes

  • Using today’s teaser rate without stress‑testing (rates can change).
  • Ignoring lock‑in/clawback or partial prepayment penalties when planning extra payments.
  • Comparing monthly installment only, without checking total interest over tenure.
  • Forgetting other housing costs (insurance, maintenance, taxes) when budgeting affordability.
  • Standard amortisation schedule (reducing balance).
  • Excludes bank fees, lock-in clawbacks, legal subsidies, and partial prepayment penalties.
  • Use as a planning model; confirm with your bank’s loan package.
