Editorial policy

Ownership Guide is a model‑driven site. We publish calculators and decision guides for Singapore ownership choices (transport, property, financing). This page explains how we create, maintain, and review content.

What we mean by “planning model”

  • Calculators here are built for decision planning: quick, transparent, conservative by default.
  • They are not legal, tax, or financial advice. Final numbers depend on your bank, lawyer, agent, and official computations.

How we build calculators

  • First principles: We model cashflows and constraints (loan caps, duties, fees) in a way users can understand.
  • Assumption transparency: Each calculator includes assumptions, a worked example, and common pitfalls.
  • Conservative mode: When uncertain, we recommend users test a conservative scenario.

Sources and updates

  • Where possible, we reference official sources (e.g., IRAS for stamp duty, CPF Board for CPF refund mechanics).
  • We update calculators when rules or common market conventions change, and we mark pages with a “Last updated” date.

Quality checks

  • Link integrity: we run sweeps to avoid broken links and duplicated paths.
  • Consistency: shared UI components (buttons, cards, headers/footers) are kept consistent site‑wide.
  • Duplication control: we prefer upgrading existing pages rather than creating overlapping topics.

Feedback

If you spot an error or want a new calculator, contact us via the contact page. Include the page URL and what you expected to see.

How we decide what to publish

We prioritise pages that help Singapore readers make expensive decisions with clear numbers: property affordability, cash + CPF planning, stamp duties, mortgage mechanics, and car total cost of ownership.

Quality checklist

  • Unique purpose: each page answers one specific question; we avoid duplicates.
  • Explicit assumptions: rates, horizons, and cost items are stated clearly.
  • Worked examples: we include at least one example so readers can sanity‑check outputs.
  • References: where official sources exist (IRAS, CPF Board, HDB), we link to them.
  • Maintenance: we update calculators when policy/rates change, and we log “last updated”.

How we avoid “thin content”

Tools alone are not enough. Every calculator is paired with explanatory content: assumptions, worked examples, common mistakes and FAQs. This helps readers use outputs correctly and makes pages useful even when you don’t run the calculator.

Corrections

If you find an error, contact us with the page URL and the official source. We review and update quickly, and we note major changes in the page’s “last updated” section.

Sources we rely on

  • IRAS (stamp duties, property transaction taxes)
  • CPF Board (CPF usage and accrued interest mechanics)
  • HDB and bank documents for HDB vs bank loan comparisons
