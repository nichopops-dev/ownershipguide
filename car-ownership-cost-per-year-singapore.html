How Much Does It Cost to Own a Car in Singapore Per Year? (2026)

Last updated: February 2026

The average cost to own a car in Singapore is typically between $24,000 to $42,000 per year, depending on car type, COE cycle, and usage.

That translates to roughly $2,000 to $3,500+ per month in true economic cost terms.

Quick Annual Cost Snapshot

Profile Estimated Annual Cost
Lower exposure (smaller car, lower COE cycle) $24,000 – $28,000
Mid exposure (typical Cat A/B range) $30,000 – $36,000
Higher exposure (larger car, high COE cycle) $38,000 – $42,000+

These figures include depreciation, insurance, fuel, maintenance, parking, ERP and opportunity cost.

What Makes Up the Annual Cost?

1) Depreciation (Largest Component)

Depreciation — largely driven by COE decay — usually accounts for the biggest share of annual cost. In many profiles, this alone ranges between $15,000–$25,000 per year.

See: COE Cost in Singapore (2026)

2) Insurance

Insurance typically ranges from $1,200 to $3,000+ per year depending on driver profile.

Detailed breakdown: Car Insurance Cost in Singapore

3) Fuel

Fuel costs vary by usage, but many drivers spend $2,500–$4,000 annually.

4) Maintenance & Repairs

Routine servicing and wear-and-tear often range $1,000–$3,000 per year, with higher variability for older vehicles.

5) Parking & ERP

Season parking + workplace parking + ERP can add $2,000–$5,000 annually.

Why Annual Framing Can Be Misleading

Looking only at annual cost hides two key risks:

  • Liquidity commitment (upfront capital tied into COE + downpayment)
  • Cycle timing risk (buying during high COE cycle amplifies exposure)
