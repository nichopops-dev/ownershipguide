How Much Does It Cost to Own a Car in Singapore Per Year? (2026)

Last updated: February 2026

In 2026, the annual cost of owning a car in Singapore typically ranges between $24,000 and $42,000+ per year, depending on COE cycle, car profile, and usage level.

That translates to roughly $2,000 to $3,500+ per month in true economic cost terms — after pricing depreciation properly.

Quick Annual Cost Snapshot (2026)

Ownership Profile Estimated Annual Cost Estimated Monthly Equivalent
Lower exposure (smaller car, lower COE cycle) $24,000 – $28,000 $2,000 – $2,300

These figures include depreciation, COE impact, insurance, fuel, maintenance, parking, ERP and opportunity cost.

What Makes Up the Annual Cost of a Car in Singapore?

1) Depreciation (Largest Cost Component)

Depreciation — largely driven by COE decay — is usually the biggest annual cost. In many ownership profiles, this alone ranges between $15,000–$25,000 per year.

If you do not understand COE structure, your annual estimate will be wrong. See: COE Cost in Singapore (2026)

2) Insurance

Insurance typically ranges from $1,200 to $3,000+ per year, depending on age, driving record and vehicle type.

Full breakdown: Car Insurance Cost in Singapore

3) Fuel

Fuel costs vary by mileage, but many drivers spend $2,500–$4,000 annually. High daily usage increases this meaningfully.

4) Maintenance & Repairs

Routine servicing and wear-and-tear typically range $1,000–$3,000 per year, with higher variability for older vehicles.

5) Parking & ERP

Season parking + workplace parking + ERP can add $2,000–$5,000 per year. Urban heavy users may exceed this.

Why “Per Year” Can Understate True Exposure

Looking only at yearly cost hides two structural risks:

  • Upfront capital commitment (downpayment + COE embedded value)
  • COE cycle timing risk (buying during peak cycles increases downside)

The clean way to think about ownership is a 5-year horizon, not 12 months.

Annual Cost vs Ride-Hailing: When Does Ownership Make Sense?

If your annual ride-hailing spend exceeds ~$30,000–$36,000, ownership may begin to make rational sense.

Below that level, ride-hailing often wins financially.

Quick Affordability Rule (Income Reality Check)

A practical capital allocation guideline:

Total transport cost should not exceed 15–20% of gross income.

If your annual car cost is $36,000, your gross income should ideally exceed $180,000 per year for the decision to remain financially comfortable.
