In Singapore, car financing is rarely about “lowest instalment”. It’s about fragility: can you survive rate shocks, cash shocks, and an exit decision without becoming trapped?

Recommended path (10 minutes)

  1. Pick the structure: loan vs cash → balloon vs normal → lease vs buy.
  2. Stress-test exposure: compare true monthly cost (not instalment) and your buffer.
  3. Check the failure mode: can you exit if COE/prices move against you?

Common traps

  • Instalment illusion: a lower monthly payment can hide a worse total exposure.
  • Balloon cliff: you’re buying a forced decision later (refinance, sell, or pay the balloon).
  • Thin buffer: you “win” the loan rate but lose when repairs/insurance/income shocks hit.
  • COE exit risk: financing is fine until you can’t exit when prices move against you.

How we model car financing in Singapore

Car financing decisions usually feel like “monthly payment vs affordability”, but the real risk is total cost + rate sensitivity. This page is designed to help you sanity-check a loan offer and understand the hidden levers.

  • Downpayment & tenure: longer tenures lower monthly payments but can raise total interest and reduce flexibility.
  • Rate sensitivity: small APR differences compound; compare total interest, not just headline instalment.
  • COE & depreciation reality: loan structure doesn’t change depreciation — it changes cashflow timing.
