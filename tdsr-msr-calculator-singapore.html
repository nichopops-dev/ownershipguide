What this is for: estimate your maximum housing loan under the two common borrowing gates:

  • TDSR: total monthly debt obligations (including this housing loan) capped as % of gross monthly income.
  • MSR: housing instalment alone capped as % of gross monthly income (commonly relevant for HDB / new EC style cases).

This is a planning calculator. Banks may apply additional haircuts, floor rates, age/tenure caps, and internal policies. If you want the mental model first: TDSR & MSR explained.

How to use this (without fooling yourself)

  1. Ratios are necessary, not sufficient. Passing TDSR/MSR doesn’t mean the purchase is smart — it just means it might be allowable.
  2. Stress-test interest rates. If your plan breaks at +1% p.a., it’s fragile.

Key assumptions (what this model does and doesn’t do)

  • TDSR caps total monthly debt obligations as a % of gross income; MSR caps housing loan repayment for certain property types.
  • Your bank’s stressed interest rate assumption and your other debts change the maximum loan materially.
  • Use this as a planning ceiling, not a promise of approval.

Common mistakes

  • Mixing up cash vs CPF (they behave differently in approvals and refunds).
  • Using optimistic assumptions (resale value, appreciation, investment return) without testing a conservative scenario.
  • Forgetting one-off costs (fees, penalties, duties) that dominate short horizons.

FAQ

Does credit card spending count as debt?

Minimum payment obligations can be treated as debt. Use a conservative estimate if unsure.

Is MSR always applicable?

MSR applies to certain purchases (commonly HDB and EC). Check your scenario.

Why does a small debt reduce max loan a lot?

Because loans are sized off monthly instalment; small changes in available instalment compound into large principal changes.

Calculator notes

Inputs explained

  • Income: use the amount that will be recognised for credit assessment (often lower than your gross).
  • Obligations: include every monthly commitment, not just the new loan.
  • Stress rate: banks may use a higher assessment rate than your offered rate.

This calculator works best together with your property affordability, cash-needed, and sale-proceeds estimates. Borrowing power is only one side of the decision. Good planning combines debt capacity, liquidity, and post-purchase resilience.

How to use this with other tools

  • Using the top borrowing number as the default target.
  • Ignoring non-mortgage cashflow strain after purchase.
  • Not checking what happens if rates stay high for longer.
  • Confusing regulatory affordability with personal affordability.

Common mistakes

Scenario C: variable-income household. Run the inputs using a weaker income month or a conservative average. Stable decisions are built on conservative assumptions, not peak months.

Scenario B: upgrade buyer. Even if ratios look fine, the move can still be fragile if your current property exit assumptions are too optimistic.

Scenario A: first-time buyer. Use the calculator to avoid sizing your purchase solely around maximum approval. A lower target can preserve buffer for renovation and early ownership surprises.

Scenario library

TDSR is broader and captures overall debt service capacity. MSR is narrower and can be binding in certain owner-occupied housing contexts. When both matter, the tighter one effectively governs your plan, which is why a purchase can look affordable in one frame but still fail in another.

TDSR vs MSR in practical terms

A borrower can technically fit a ratio and still end up feeling cashflow-tight because childcare, family support, renovation, travel, or lifestyle commitments were not fully respected. That is why your own comfort threshold matters. The strongest use of this calculator is to identify a safer internal borrowing ceiling, not just the outer legal or bank limit.

What the ratios do not tell you

  • Separate “can borrow” from “should borrow”. A maximum borrowing number is not the same as a comfortable borrowing number.
  • Stress test the rate. A loan that only works at today’s rate is a weak plan.
  • Remember total housing cost. Mortgage is not the whole monthly burden; add maintenance, tax, insurance, and renovation financing where relevant.

How to use this result wisely

TDSR and MSR are usually discussed as if they are just bank rules, but in practice they are decision filters. They affect what you can borrow, what you should borrow, and whether your housing plan remains comfortable after normal life spending. This calculator is not just for seeing whether you may pass a ratio test. It is for understanding whether the loan you are aiming for fits your broader life.

What this calculator is really helping you estimate

The best calculator result is not the most flattering one. It is the one that still helps when reality is slightly worse than expected. Use conservative assumptions, compare at least two scenarios, and focus on decisions that remain workable without perfect conditions.
