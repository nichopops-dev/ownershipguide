Property Financing in Singapore (2026): Home Loans, Refinancing, Borrowing Rules

Property financing decisions in Singapore are long-duration leverage decisions. They affect not just your monthly instalment but your borrowing capacity, liquidity, rate sensitivity, exit flexibility, and the total amount of interest and friction you will absorb over time.

This hub groups the property financing pages so you can move through them in the right order: borrowing rules, LTV and approval mechanics, cash friction, loan choice, then refinance or restructuring decisions.

Cash, Duties, Grants, and Financing Friction

Many buyers focus on the loan but underprice the friction around the loan. Upfront cash, stamp duties, valuation gaps, CPF effects, renovation, and future selling costs all affect whether the financing plan is genuinely sustainable. Once the financing works on paper, check the ongoing drag as well: property tax, condo maintenance / MCST fees, and home insurance versus fire insurance all change how comfortable the purchase feels after completion.

Moving, Upgrading, and Sell-Buy Sequencing

Property financing becomes more fragile when you are not just buying, but moving from one property to another. The monthly instalment may still be fine while the transition itself becomes the real risk. Sale proceeds, CPF refund timing, option money, duties, and completion mismatch can all create pressure before the next home is fully secured. The early buy-side execution pages on option fee / exercise fee, conveyancing / legal fees, and bank valuation help fill in the narrower gaps that broad affordability pages do not isolate well.
