Car Insurance Cost in Singapore (2026): What You’ll Actually Pay

Last updated: February 2026

In Singapore, car insurance is not “a few hundred dollars a year” unless you are a low-risk driver with a low-risk vehicle. For most owners, comprehensive insurance typically sits in the $1,500–$2,500 per year range, and can be materially higher for younger drivers, higher-value cars, or drivers with claims history.

This page shows (1) realistic premium ranges, (2) what actually drives your price, and (3) how to model insurance properly inside your ownership decision.

1) The “Realistic Range” Most Owners Fall Into

For everyday private car ownership in Singapore, comprehensive insurance commonly lands here:

Driver / Profile Typical Annual Premium (SGD) Why it shifts
Low-risk driver, mass-market car $1,200 – $1,800 Good NCD, stable profile, lower repair exposure
Average driver, average car $1,500 – $2,500 Most common “normal” band
Higher-risk / higher-value profile $2,500 – $4,000+ Younger driver, claims, expensive parts, performance models

These are simplified bands to help decision-making. Actual quotations vary by insurer and your exact inputs.

2) What Determines Your Premium (In Practice)

Most people guess wrong. These are the drivers that move your premium materially:

  • NCD (No-Claim Discount): the biggest lever over time. Claims can reset or reduce NCD.
  • Driver age and experience: younger / less experienced drivers pay more.
  • Car model and repairability: expensive parts, complex repairs, and higher theft risk increase premiums.
  • Sum insured / car value: higher replacement exposure = higher premium.
  • Claims history: prior claims (even small) often push up renewal pricing.
  • Named drivers / usage pattern: additional drivers can increase risk exposure.

The key point: insurance is not a fixed cost. It is a risk price that can jump after one “small incident”.

3) The Common Mistake: Under-budgeting Insurance

Most owners mentally budget insurance as: “around $100/month”. That’s often wrong.

If your annual premium is $2,400, insurance alone is: $200/month.

That matters because your “monthly cost of owning a car” is usually underestimated. If you want the complete monthly model (not just instalment), read: True Monthly Cost of Owning a Car in Singapore.

4) How Insurance Fits Into a 5-Year Ownership Decision

Insurance is best modelled as a 5-year exposure, not a 1-year annoyance.

A simple 5-year projection:

  • Low band: $1,200/year → $6,000 over 5 years
  • Normal band: $2,000/year → $10,000 over 5 years
  • Higher band: $3,500/year → $17,500 over 5 years

Insurance is not the biggest cost (depreciation is), but it is a consistent drag that stacks. For the full 5-year ownership framework, see: The Real Cost of Owning a Car in Singapore (5-Year Breakdown).

5) When Insurance Cost Should Change Your Decision

Insurance cost changes decisions when it pushes your true monthly cost into ride-hailing territory.

  • If your insurance is consistently above ~$250/month, your ownership cost structure is already “high exposure”.
  • If you’re borderline on car vs ride-hailing, insurance is one of the easiest costs to mis-estimate.

If you want the clean break-even check (based on your monthly ride-hailing spend), use: Car vs Ride-Hailing Break-Even Calculator.

6) A Simple “Quick Estimate” You Can Use

If you want a conservative planning number without overthinking:

  • Planning floor: $1,500/year (~$125/month)
  • Typical planning number: $2,000–$2,500/year (~$167–$208/month)
  • Higher-risk planning: $3,000+/year (~$250+/month)

The goal is not perfect accuracy. The goal is avoiding a distorted ownership decision.
