Car Insurance Cost in Singapore (2026): What You’ll Actually Pay

Last updated: February 2026

Car insurance in Singapore is rarely “a few hundred dollars a year.” For most private car owners, comprehensive insurance typically sits in the $1,500–$2,500 per year range — and can be materially higher for younger drivers, higher-value cars, or drivers with claims history.

Insurance is not just a compliance requirement. It is a recurring cost that compounds over your full ownership cycle.

1) Realistic Premium Ranges (2026)

For everyday private car ownership:

Driver / Profile Typical Annual Premium (SGD) Why It Shifts
Low-risk driver, mass-market car $1,200 – $1,800 High NCD, stable risk profile
Average driver, average car $1,500 – $2,500 Most common approval band
Higher-risk / higher-value profile $2,500 – $4,000+ Younger driver, prior claims, expensive repair exposure

These are simplified modelling bands. Actual quotes vary by insurer and exact inputs.

2) What Actually Drives Your Insurance Price

  • No-Claim Discount (NCD): largest long-term pricing lever.
  • Driver age & experience: younger drivers pay significantly more.
  • Car repair cost & model risk: expensive parts = higher premiums.
  • Claims history: even minor claims may increase renewal rates.
  • Sum insured: higher replacement value increases risk exposure.
  • Named drivers: additional risk layers increase premium.

Insurance is a dynamic risk price — not a fixed line item.

3) The Common Budgeting Mistake

Many buyers mentally allocate: “around $100/month.”

If your premium is $2,400/year, insurance alone equals: $200/month.

4) 5-Year Insurance Cost Exposure

Insurance should be modelled across your intended ownership horizon.

  • $1,200/year → $6,000 over 5 years
  • $2,000/year → $10,000 over 5 years
  • $3,500/year → $17,500 over 5 years

While depreciation remains the largest cost, insurance is a persistent annual drag.

5) When Insurance Cost Should Change Your Decision

  • If insurance exceeds ~$250/month, total exposure is already elevated.
  • If you're near the break-even point vs ride-hailing, insurance often tips the scale.

6) Simple Conservative Planning Numbers

  • Planning floor: $1,500/year (~$125/month)
  • Typical planning number: $2,000–$2,500/year (~$167–$208/month)
  • Higher-risk planning: $3,000+/year (~$250+/month)

The objective is not precision. The objective is avoiding distorted ownership decisions.
