The Real Cost of Owning a Car in Singapore (5-Year Breakdown)

Last updated: February 2026

Car ownership in Singapore is not just a monthly instalment decision. It is a multi-year capital commitment involving depreciation, financing cost, insurance, fuel, maintenance, parking and opportunity cost.

This article provides a structured 5-year ownership view. If you prefer a detailed monthly breakdown, see: True Monthly Cost of Owning a Car in Singapore .

1. The Full 5-Year Cost Structure

  • Purchase price & COE exposure
  • Depreciation
  • Loan interest (if financed)
  • Insurance
  • Fuel
  • Maintenance & repairs
  • Parking & ERP
  • Opportunity cost of upfront capital

Ignoring any one of these leads to underestimating real ownership cost.

2. Singapore Example (2026 Conditions)

The following ranges reflect typical private car ownership conditions in Singapore. Actual numbers depend on vehicle category and usage patterns.

Purchase & Depreciation

A mass-market car may cost $120,000–$180,000 inclusive of COE. Over 5 years, depreciation commonly ranges between:

$70,000 – $110,000

Loan Interest

Depending on loan size and interest rate, total interest over 5 years may range between:

$10,000 – $30,000

Insurance

Comprehensive insurance typically costs:

$7,500 – $12,500 over 5 years

Fuel

Fuel cost varies by mileage, but commonly totals:

$12,000 – $18,000 over 5 years

Maintenance & Repairs

Routine servicing, tyres, wear-and-tear and occasional repairs may total:

$8,000 – $15,000 over 5 years

Parking & ERP

Season parking, workplace parking and ERP can accumulate to:

$9,000 – $20,000 over 5 years

Opportunity Cost

If $30,000–$50,000 of upfront capital is committed and assumed at a 5% annual return, the opportunity cost over 5 years may be:

$7,500 – $15,000+

4. When Car Ownership Makes Financial Sense

  • High monthly ride-hailing usage ($2,500–$3,000+)
  • Schedule-critical family logistics
  • Stable income and strong liquidity buffer
