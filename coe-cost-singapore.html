COE Cost in Singapore (2026): Depreciation, Cycle Risk & 5-Year Exposure

Last updated: February 2026

The Certificate of Entitlement (COE) is not a simple “tax”. It is the largest structural cost component of owning a car in Singapore.

In many ownership profiles, COE alone accounts for 50–70% of total depreciation exposure over a 5-year holding period.

1) What Is COE (Structurally)?

A COE grants the right to own and use a vehicle in Singapore for 10 years. It is awarded via a bidding system and fluctuates with supply and demand.

For most private car buyers, exposure is primarily Category A or Category B. The key insight is not category differences — it is understanding that COE embeds a large capital commitment into your purchase price.

2) COE Price Cycles (Volatility Is Structural)

COE prices are highly cyclical. Over the past decade, broad ranges have looked like:

  • Lower cycles: ~$30,000 – $50,000
  • Mid cycles: ~$60,000 – $90,000
  • High spikes: $100,000+

These illustrate structural volatility — not guarantees. Always reference current bidding results before committing capital.

3) Why COE Drives Depreciation

When you buy a $150,000 car in Singapore, a large portion of that price is COE.

Over 5 years, depreciation is effectively:

COE decay + vehicle value decline.

Example:

  • COE component: $100,000
  • 5 years consumed: ~50% of COE lifespan
  • Implied COE usage cost: ~$50,000

That single component can exceed total ride-hailing cost for many households.

4) The “10-Year” Illusion

Many buyers anchor mentally on “10 years”. In practice, most owners sell within 3–6 years.

That means COE cost is consumed faster than expected — and early exits during weak COE cycles can compress resale value further.

6) When COE Should Change Your Decision

  • Buying during extreme price spikes increases downside exposure.
  • Short holding periods amplify COE timing risk.
  • If ride-hailing spend is borderline, high COE cycles often tilt decisions away from ownership.

7) Final Perspective

COE is not just a line item. It is a macro-level capital allocation decision embedded inside every car purchase.

The higher the COE cycle, the higher your depreciation risk and capital exposure. That is the real cost most buyers underestimate.
