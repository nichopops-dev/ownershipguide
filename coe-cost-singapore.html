COE Cost in Singapore (2026): What You’re Really Paying For

Last updated: February 2026

The Certificate of Entitlement (COE) is not a “tax”. It is the single largest structural cost component of owning a car in Singapore.

In many cases, COE alone accounts for 50–70% of total depreciation exposure over a 5-year ownership period.

1) What Is COE, Structurally?

A COE grants the right to own and use a vehicle in Singapore for 10 years. It is awarded via a bidding system and fluctuates based on supply and demand.

There are different categories (A, B, Open Category), but for most private car buyers, the relevant exposure is Category A or B.

2) Typical COE Price Ranges (Volatile by Nature)

COE prices are highly cyclical. Over the past decade, they have ranged broadly:

  • Lower cycle periods: ~$30,000 – $50,000
  • Mid-cycle range: ~$60,000 – $90,000
  • High cycle spikes: $100,000+

These ranges illustrate structural volatility. Always refer to current bidding data before decision-making.

3) Why COE Is the Core Depreciation Driver

When you buy a $150,000 car in Singapore, a large portion of that cost is COE.

Over 5 years, depreciation is not just “car value loss”. It is effectively:

COE decay + vehicle value decline.

Example:

  • COE component: $100,000
  • 5 years consumed: ~50% of COE lifespan
  • Implied COE usage cost: ~$50,000

That alone can exceed total ride-hailing cost for many households.

4) The 10-Year Illusion

Many buyers mentally anchor on “10 years”. But most owners sell within 3–6 years.

That means you are consuming COE value at an accelerated capital rate.

If you exit early during a weak COE cycle, resale value may compress further.

7) When COE Cycle Should Change Your Decision

  • Buying during extreme price spikes increases downside exposure.
  • Buying during lower cycle periods reduces depreciation risk.
  • Short holding periods amplify COE risk.

If your ride-hailing cost is borderline, high COE cycles often tilt the decision away from ownership.

Final Perspective

COE is not just a line item. It is a macro-level capital allocation decision.

The higher the COE cycle, the higher your embedded depreciation risk. That is the real cost most buyers underestimate.
