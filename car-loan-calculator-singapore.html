How to interpret this

  1. Separate “can pay” from “should pay”. An instalment can be affordable while total ownership cost is still a bad trade.
  2. Use the stress test. Pair this with the car affordability calculator (true monthly cost, not just instalment).
  3. Watch tenure creep. A longer tenure reduces instalment but can increase your total interest exposure.

Car loan (flat vs effective): why this matters

Many Singapore car loans are advertised with a flat rate. Flat rates understate true cost because interest is effectively front‑loaded. Use this tool to compare loans on a like‑for‑like basis (monthly + total interest).

Quick checks

  • Compare two offers using total paid, not just rate.
  • Include fees — they can flip the decision.
  • Stress test your budget: what if your income dips for 3 months?

Worked example

Run one base case and one conservative case. For example, increase the key cost (rate/maintenance/vacancy) by 20% and reduce resale by 10%. If the winner stays the same, your decision is robust.

Methodology & assumptions

This calculator is a decision model — it helps you sanity-check trade-offs and stress-test your downside. It is not a quote from a bank/dealer, and it does not include every edge-case fee.

How to use it:

  • Use the actual on-the-road price (after rebates) and your intended downpayment.
  • Try two tenures: a “comfortable” one and a shorter one — then compare total interest cost.
  • If your income fluctuates, stress-test with a higher interest rate to see if the instalment becomes fragile.

What the model includes (and does not):

  • Includes: the core cashflow/interest mechanics relevant to car loan affordability and total interest.
  • Does not include: promotional packages, negotiated rebates, or your personal credit terms — use your own numbers where possible.
