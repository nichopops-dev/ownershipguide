Refinance vs Reprice (Singapore Home Loan, 2026)

Decision comparison · Last updated: February 2026

This is a break-even + optionality decision. Don’t chase the lowest headline rate. Decide on all-in savings vs all-in costs over your realistic horizon.

Mortgage interest mechanics Refinance savings calculator Start here

Decision checklist

  • Rate delta: Is the new rate meaningfully better, and for how long?
  • Costs: Legal/subsidy, valuation, clawbacks, and fees included?
  • Lock-in: Are you still in lock-in, or will penalties wipe out the gain?
  • Optionality: Do you want to reset structure (tenure/package), or just reprice?

TL;DR

  • Try reprice first if your bank can get “close enough” without legal hassle.
  • Refinance when the market offers a clear step-change (rate + terms) and your bank won’t match.
  • The only math you need: break-even months = switching cost ÷ monthly saving.

The decision in one line

  • If you can reduce rate meaningfully with minimal friction: reprice.
  • If you need a big step-down in rate/terms improvement: refinance.

Break-even in 60 seconds

Monthly interest saving ≈ outstanding balance × (rate gap) ÷ 12.

Break-even months ≈ total one-off costs ÷ monthly saving.

Rule of thumb: if you break even within ~6–18 months, it’s usually worth serious consideration.

Cost checklist (what people forget)

  • Legal fees (usually refinance; repricing often avoids this)
  • Valuation fee (sometimes required)
  • Admin / processing fees
  • Cash rebates / subsidies and clawback terms
  • Lock-in, early redemption penalties, notice periods
  • Effective rate after promos (compare steady-state, not teaser-only)

Decision rules that work in practice

  • Reprice wins when your bank is within ~0.20–0.40% of best market and friction is low.
  • Refinance wins when you can lock in a meaningful improvement and you’re not exiting soon.
  • If you may sell/upgrade within 12–24 months, prioritise optionality over squeezing basis points.

Pitfalls that destroy savings

  • Ignoring lock-in and early redemption penalties.
  • Missing subsidy clawbacks or package conditions.
  • Optimising the rate but increasing fragility (no buffer for rate shock).

FAQ

Reprice first or refinance first?

Default: ask your current bank to reprice first. If they won’t move enough, refinance is worth the paperwork.

What rate gap is “worth it”?

It depends on balance and true switching costs. 0.30–0.60% on a large balance can be meaningful quickly.

What if rates are about to drop?

Avoid long lock-ins unless the discount is large. Choose packages that preserve exit optionality.

Refinance vs reprice: what actually matters

Refinancing is not automatically ‘better’. The best move is the one with the fastest break-even and the least future regret.

Decision checklist:

  • Compute break-even months after legal/admin costs and any subsidy clawback.
  • Check whether the new package introduces a new lock-in that reduces flexibility.
  • If rates are unstable, optimise for optionality: shorter lock-in, clear repricing path.
