Interpretation

If your instalment is close to the cap, your plan is fragile. Property is a multi-year capital structure decision. Don’t ignore interest drag, setup shock, and exit friction.

How to Interpret the Output

  • Safe: instalment ≤ 30% of gross income (and you still keep buffers).
  • Borderline: 30%–35% of gross income (works only with strong buffer + stable income).
  • Risky: > 35% of gross income (fragile under rates, job risk, or life events).

Borrowing gates are only step 1. Your real outcomes are driven by: CPF refund mechanics and net proceeds reality.

FAQ

Does this replace a bank approval?

No. Treat it as a planning tool. Banks may haircut income, include all debts, and use stress rates that differ from your assumptions.

Why is my max loan lower than expected?

TDSR includes existing debts and banks apply stress rates. Read: TDSR & MSR guide.

Does this include renovation and buffers?

No. This is a borrowing and upfront exposure stress test. Use cash required and renovation planning for the full stack.

Methodology & assumptions

This calculator is a decision model — it helps you sanity-check trade-offs and stress-test your downside. It is not a quote from a bank/dealer, and it does not include every edge-case fee.

How to use it:

  • Input your household income and target price, then test different loan amounts and interest rates.
  • Use a stress rate that feels uncomfortable — the point is to see whether the decision survives reality.
  • Consider non-mortgage costs (maintenance, insurance, renovation, agent/legal) as separate buffers.

What the model includes (and does not):

  • Includes: the core cashflow/interest mechanics relevant to property affordability stress-testing.
  • Does not include: promotional packages, negotiated rebates, or your personal credit terms — use your own numbers where possible.
