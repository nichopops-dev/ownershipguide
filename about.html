About Ownership Guide

Structured ownership cost models for Singapore (2026).

Ownership Guide is an independent, model-driven website focused on the real cost of ownership in Singapore. We publish structured breakdowns for high-commitment decisions — especially where the biggest costs are hidden inside depreciation, liquidity impact, financing drag, and opportunity cost.

Core principle: monthly instalments are not the same as true cost.

Most ownership mistakes happen because decisions are made using a single visible number (the monthly payment), while the real exposure sits in depreciation curves, COE timing, resale liquidity, and risk volatility.

What We Cover

  • Transport: car ownership exposure, COE mechanics, insurance reality, financing drag, ride-hailing break-even
  • Property: ownership structures, acquisition + financing exposure, liquidity and opportunity cost
  • Capital allocation: trade-offs between committing capital vs preserving flexibility

Singapore is the starting dataset because its ownership costs behave differently (COE, financing constraints, stamp duties, etc.). The modelling framework is designed to be extendable.

How the Models Work

Each article is written as a decision framework. We aim to be transparent about assumptions and consistent in structure so pages are comparable.

  • Cost structure mapping: what actually drives the decision
  • 5-year exposure lens: total capital risk over time (not 1-month affordability)
  • Liquidity + volatility framing: what breaks in the real world (repairs, renewal jumps, cycle timing)
  • Decision checkpoints: clear gates to avoid common traps

Figures shown are simplified planning models — not quotes. Always validate with current COE results, actual loan offers, and insurer quotations.

What this site is

Ownership Guide is a Singapore “ownership decision engine”: we publish calculators and decision guides for transport, property, and financing choices. The goal is simple — turn confusing trade-offs into clear numbers.

How to interpret calculators

Most tools here are planning models. They make assumptions explicit so you can pressure-test scenarios quickly. For final numbers, confirm with your bank/lawyer/agent and official calculators.

How we maintain quality

  • We prefer upgrading existing pages over creating overlapping topics.
  • We add worked examples, assumptions, and FAQs to help you use outputs correctly.
  • We keep references where official sources exist (IRAS, CPF Board, etc.).
