Car Leasing vs Buying in Singapore: Which Is Cheaper? (2026)

Last updated: February 2026

Car leasing is often marketed as “lower commitment” and “lower upfront cost.” Buying is often framed as “better long-term value.”

The real comparison is financial exposure, flexibility, and total cost over time.

1. What Is Car Leasing in Singapore?

Car leasing typically involves:

  • Fixed monthly payment
  • No ownership of the vehicle
  • Maintenance often included
  • Shorter commitment (1–5 years)

Leasing removes depreciation risk but includes profit margin for the leasing company.

2. What Does Buying Involve?

Buying includes:

  • Upfront capital or loan financing
  • Full depreciation exposure
  • Responsibility for insurance and maintenance
  • Asset ownership

For full ownership modelling, see: The Real Cost of Owning a Car in Singapore .

3. Typical Cost Comparison (Singapore Example)

Leasing Example

Monthly lease: $1,800 – $2,800 Term: 3 years

Total 3-year cost: $64,800 – $100,800

After 3 years: no asset retained.

Buying Example (3-Year View)

Using mid-range ownership modelling:

  • Depreciation over 3 years: ~$45,000 – $60,000
  • Insurance: ~$4,500 – $7,500
  • Fuel: ~$7,500 – $10,000
  • Maintenance: ~$4,000 – $8,000
  • Parking & ERP: ~$5,000 – $12,000

Estimated 3-year ownership exposure: $66,000 – $97,000+

But after 3 years, you still own the vehicle with residual value.

4. When Leasing Makes Financial Sense

  • You need short-term flexibility (1–3 years)
  • You want predictable monthly payments
  • You do not want depreciation risk
  • You lack large upfront capital

5. When Buying Makes Financial Sense

  • You plan to keep the vehicle long-term
  • You can manage depreciation exposure
  • You want asset control
  • You can finance at reasonable rates

6. Financial Comparison Summary

Factor Leasing Buying
Upfront Capital Low High
Depreciation Risk None (transferred) Fully borne
Flexibility Higher Lower
Long-Term Cost Efficiency Lower Higher (if managed well)
