True Monthly Cost of Owning a Car in Singapore (2026 Breakdown)

Last updated: February 2026

The most common mistake in Singapore is using the loan instalment as the “cost of owning a car.” Instalments are just the payment method. The true monthly cost is driven by depreciation (COE exposure), plus insurance, fuel, maintenance, parking/ERP and the opportunity cost of your upfront capital.

Quick Answer (Realistic Monthly Range)

  • Lower exposure: ~$1,900–$2,300/month
  • Typical ownership band: ~$2,300–$3,200/month
  • Higher exposure: ~$3,200–$3,500+/month

These are simplified decision bands. Your actual cost depends on depreciation profile, loan size/tenure, insurer pricing, and mileage.

Fast check: If your monthly ride-hailing spend is consistently in the same zone, ownership may be rational.

1) The Monthly Cost Categories (All-In)

  • Depreciation (often the largest)
  • Loan interest (if financed)
  • Insurance
  • Fuel
  • Maintenance & repairs
  • Parking & ERP
  • Opportunity cost of upfront capital

Ignoring any single component produces a distorted monthly number — and most people ignore depreciation and opportunity cost.

2) Depreciation (The Real Cost Engine)

In Singapore, depreciation is the real cost engine because COE sits inside it. A simple way to estimate depreciation per month:

Depreciation per month = (Purchase price − Estimated resale value) ÷ months held

Example: If a car costs $150,000 and its projected value after 5 years is $80,000:

$70,000 over 5 years → ~$1,167 per month

3) Loan Interest (If You Finance)

Interest cost is often underestimated because car loans are quoted using flat rates, not true EIR. Depending on loan size and rates, interest can add:

$200–$600 per month

If you want to understand flat rate vs effective rate properly: Car Loan Rates in Singapore (2026).

4) Insurance

Typical comprehensive insurance:

$1,500–$2,500 per year → ~$125–$210 per month

Driver age, claim history, and car repair exposure move this materially. Full breakdown: Car Insurance Cost in Singapore (2026).

5) Fuel

Fuel is usage-driven. A practical band for many private drivers:

$250–$450 per month

Your exact figure depends on mileage, vehicle efficiency and fuel prices. Treat this as a planning band.

6) Maintenance & Repairs

Routine servicing, tyres, wear-and-tear averaged over time:

$100–$250 per month (averaged)

The risk is volatility — one unexpected repair can distort your yearly average.

7) Parking & ERP

Season parking + workplace parking + incidental parking:

$150–$350 per month

ERP depends on your routes and peak-hour frequency.

8) Opportunity Cost (Upfront Capital)

If you commit $40,000 upfront and assume a 5% annual return elsewhere:

~$2,000 per year → ~$167 per month

This is rarely modelled — but financially real.
