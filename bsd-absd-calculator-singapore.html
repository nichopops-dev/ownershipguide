Assumptions (rates)

  • BSD tiers (residential): 1% (first S$180k), 2% (next S$180k), 3% (next S$640k), 4% (next S$500k), 5% (next S$1.5m), 6% (remaining) — effective 15 Feb 2023.
  • ABSD (on or after 27 Apr 2023): SC 2nd 20%, SC 3rd+ 30%; SPR 1st 5%, SPR 2nd 30%, SPR 3rd+ 35%; Foreigner 60%; Entity 65%; Trustee 65%.

Rates can change; confirm on IRAS before committing.

How to use this

This page is a planning model. Use it to get a directionally correct answer fast, then confirm final numbers with your bank/lawyer/agent and official calculators where relevant.

Key assumptions (what this model does and doesn’t do)

  • BSD is tiered (marginal) — higher portions of the price are taxed at higher rates.
  • ABSD depends on buyer profile (citizenship, number of properties, etc.).
  • This is a planning estimate; confirm with IRAS for your exact profile and date of purchase.

Worked example

Example: why BSD isn’t ‘one flat %’

BSD is calculated in brackets. That’s why a small increase in price doesn’t apply the top rate to the whole price — only the portion in that bracket.

Common mistakes

  • Mixing up cash vs CPF (they behave differently in approvals and refunds).
  • Using optimistic assumptions (resale value, appreciation, investment return) without testing a conservative scenario.
  • Forgetting one-off costs (fees, penalties, duties) that dominate short horizons.

FAQ

Does ABSD apply to my case?

It depends on your profile and existing property count. Check IRAS guidance for the exact category.

Do I pay BSD/ABSD in cash?

Often payable at completion; CPF usage may be possible depending on your situation.

Should I include this in my upfront cash planning?

Yes — stamp duties are one of the biggest upfront cashflow items.
