Example: why BSD isn’t ‘one flat %’

BSD is calculated in brackets. That’s why a small increase in price doesn’t apply the top rate to the whole price — only the portion in that bracket.

Common mistakes

  • Mixing up cash vs CPF (they behave differently in approvals and refunds).
  • Using optimistic assumptions (resale value, appreciation, investment return) without testing a conservative scenario.
  • Forgetting one-off costs (fees, penalties, duties) that dominate short horizons.

FAQ

Does ABSD apply to my case?

It depends on your profile and existing property count. Check IRAS guidance for the exact category.

Do I pay BSD/ABSD in cash?

Often payable at completion; CPF usage may be possible depending on your situation.

Should I include this in my upfront cash planning?

Yes — stamp duties are one of the biggest upfront cashflow items.

What this result does not include

This result focuses on stamp duties based on the assumptions you keyed in. It does not include legal fees, valuation fees, renovation costs, loan setup costs, or the cashflow effect of having to complete two linked transactions close together. That matters because buyers often treat duties as the whole transaction friction, when in practice duties are only one part of the total entry burden.

It also does not resolve edge cases around ownership structure or beneficial ownership that may need professional confirmation. Use the number here as a planning anchor, then stress-test the wider transaction stack before you commit.

How the inputs affect the output

  • Property count: depends on beneficial ownership, not just your name on title.
  • Profile: residency and marital purchase arrangements can change ABSD treatment.

How to interpret the result

Stamp duties are not just “extra cost”. They affect the amount of cash you need, the required holding period to make a move worthwhile, and whether a purchase still makes sense after all frictions are included. For some buyers, especially with ABSD exposure, the stamp duty is not a side cost — it is one of the main costs.

Why stamp duty matters beyond the headline number

BSD and ABSD are often talked about as if they are simple percentage add-ons, but they affect behaviour, feasibility, and strategy in important ways. This calculator helps you estimate the immediate stamp duty impact and understand how much the transaction friction changes the economics of the purchase.

What this calculator helps you understand

The aim is not perfection. It is to avoid being surprised by costs you could have anticipated with a slightly better planning process.

  • Use a conservative scenario, not only a comfortable scenario.
  • Test whether the decision still works if one major assumption worsens.
  • Write down what the calculator excludes so you do not treat the result as complete.
  • Prefer a slightly pessimistic planning number over an optimistic one.

How to improve the quality of your estimate

Once you know the tax cost, compare it against the expected value of the move: lifestyle improvement, rent savings, investment logic, or restructuring benefit. If the tax friction overwhelms the benefit, the move may still be possible, but it may not be smart.

What to compare after getting the result

Stamp duties can turn a superficially attractive move into a poor one, especially when the holding period is short or the investment case is thin. That is why duty calculations are not just tax admin — they are part of strategic decision-making.

Why transaction friction changes strategy

Stamp duties matter because they are paid at the transaction stage, not slowly over time. That means they can make a move look affordable on paper but still painful in practice if your buffer is thin or your holding period is short. Heavy transaction friction also punishes indecision: the more often you buy, sell, or restructure without a clear plan, the more of your capital disappears into costs that do not improve the property itself.
