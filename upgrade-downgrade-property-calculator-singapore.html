How to use this

This page is a planning model. Use it to get a directionally correct answer fast, then confirm final numbers with your bank/lawyer/agent and official calculators where relevant.

Key assumptions (what this model does and doesn’t do)

  • This is the bridge model: it compares your sell-side cash/CPF availability versus buy-side cash/CPF needs.
  • It helps spot whether your constraint is cash, CPF, or borrowing limit (TDSR/MSR).
  • Treat this as a ‘feasibility check’ before you commit to an OTP or timeline.

Worked example

Example: upgrading with high CPF refund

If CPF refund is large, your ‘cash proceeds’ may look fine but your CPF may be constrained for the next purchase depending on timing and balances. Use the pipeline/planner to sequence the steps.

Common mistakes

  • Mixing up cash vs CPF (they behave differently in approvals and refunds).
  • Using optimistic assumptions (resale value, appreciation, investment return) without testing a conservative scenario.
  • Forgetting one-off costs (fees, penalties, duties) that dominate short horizons.

FAQ

What if I sell and buy at the same time?

Timing matters. You may need bridging, temporary cash, or coordinated completion dates.

Do I need to add renovation?

Yes if meaningful — include as cash-only upfront for the buy side.

Where does TDSR/MSR fit?

This tool checks cash/CPF feasibility; use the TDSR/MSR calculator for borrowing ceiling.

Methodology & assumptions

This calculator is a decision model — it helps you sanity-check trade-offs and stress-test your downside. It is not a quote from a bank/dealer, and it does not include every edge-case fee.

How to use it:

  • Input your current home’s estimated sale proceeds and your target purchase price.
  • Stress-test with a conservative sale price and a higher purchase cost (renovation/fees).
  • Use the result as a feasibility check, then run the pipeline timing model.

What the model includes (and does not):

  • Includes: the core cashflow/interest mechanics relevant to upgrade/downgrade feasibility.
  • Does not include: promotional packages, negotiated rebates, or your personal credit terms — use your own numbers where possible.
