Best Car Loan Rates in Singapore (2026): What You’ll Actually Pay Over 5 Years

Last updated: February 2026

Car loan rates in Singapore are commonly quoted as “1.98%” or “2.48%”. What most buyers do not realise is that these are flat rates — not the true effective interest rate (EIR).

This page explains how car loans actually work, what realistic rate ranges look like in 2026, and how financing changes your real 5-year ownership cost.

1) How Car Loans Work in Singapore

Singapore car loans are typically quoted using a flat interest rate. This means interest is calculated on the original principal for the entire loan tenure.

The actual borrowing cost is better understood using the Effective Interest Rate (EIR), which is often roughly 1.8–2.2x the quoted flat rate.

Example: A 2.28% flat rate loan may translate to ~4.2%–4.5% EIR.

3) What Most Buyers Get Wrong

  • Flat rate is not the real borrowing cost.
  • Stretching tenure reduces monthly instalment but increases total interest paid.
  • 7-year loans materially increase total financing exposure.

Lower instalments do not mean lower cost.

6) Should You Finance or Pay Cash?

  • If your investment return exceeds effective borrowing cost, financing may be rational.
  • If financing stretches liquidity, risk increases materially.
  • Short holding periods increase financing drag.

Financing is not inherently bad — but it amplifies capital exposure.

7) Where To Compare Loan Rates

Major Singapore banks and financial institutions provide car loans. Always compare:

  • Quoted flat rate
  • Effective interest rate (EIR)
  • Early repayment penalties
  • Lock-in periods

The lowest advertised flat rate is not always the lowest total cost.
