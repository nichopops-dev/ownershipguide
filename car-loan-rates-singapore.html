Best Car Loan Rates in Singapore (2026): Flat Rate vs EIR Explained

Last updated: February 2026

Car loan rates in Singapore are commonly advertised as 1.98% or 2.48%. These figures are flat rates — not the true borrowing cost.

The real cost is reflected in the Effective Interest Rate (EIR), which is usually 1.8–2.2× higher than the quoted flat rate.

If you are financing a $100,000 car, even a 0.5% difference in flat rate can mean $2,000–$5,000 difference over five years.

1) How Car Loans Work in Singapore

Most Singapore car loans use a flat interest structure. Interest is calculated on the original principal for the entire tenure, instead of on a declining balance.

Example: A 2.28% flat rate may translate to ~4.2%–4.5% EIR.

This structure makes loans appear cheaper than they actually are.

4) 5-Year vs 7-Year Loan: What Changes?

  • 7-year tenure lowers monthly instalment.
  • Total interest paid increases significantly.
  • Depreciation + financing drag becomes heavier.

Lower monthly instalment does not mean lower total cost.

5) How Financing Affects Total Ownership Cost

Loan cost is only one layer of total exposure. You must also account for:

  • Depreciation (COE decay)
  • Insurance
  • Fuel
  • Maintenance
  • Parking & ERP

6) Should You Finance or Pay Cash?

  • If your investment returns exceed EIR, financing may be rational.
  • If financing stretches liquidity, risk rises.
  • Short holding periods magnify financing drag.

Financing amplifies capital exposure. It does not reduce it.

7) How to Compare Loan Offers Properly

Always compare:
  • Quoted flat rate
  • Effective interest rate (EIR)
  • Total interest over full tenure
  • Early repayment penalties
  • Lock-in period

The lowest advertised flat rate is not always the lowest total cost.
